Drink Master

Work plan and checklist for interaction between QA and Fullstack developer

1- Project planning is done with a mentor for Fullstack students (QA students are also present to understand the details of the project).
2- Calls are made by teams (FS + QA):
- select Team Leads from two directions - Team Lead Fullstack and Team Lead QA;
- Fullstack students also choose a Scrum Master for the entire team (FS + QA), one Scrum Master for all;
- The Scrum Master of the team creates a shared Trello (see Preparation materials for the project, !!! Be sure to add cards for QA “Ready for test” and “In test” !!!)
- the team enters tasks, evaluates tasks;
- the team distributes tasks among themselves;
- the team determines the place and time of daily team meetings - Stand-up (FS+QA students);
- organizes team meetings Scrum Master of the team and makes sure that all participants are present (FS+QA students).
3- Developers and testers start working on their tasks.
4- After the developers have implemented at least one of the tasks and/or sections of the project, the developer transfers this card to the "Ready for test" column in Trello and informs the Team Lead about it at the daily team meeting, the testers or/or the responsible person from the testers.
5- Testers take the cards from the "Ready for test" column to the "In test" column and work on the tasks. Distributes tasks between QA students - Team Lead testers:
- If everything is OK, the tester moves this card to the "Done" column.
- If there are bugs, the tester creates a new card in the "To do" column for developers and describes in it what needs to be improved/corrected in the current task:
- The tester reports the completed task to his Team Lead (Team Lead QA).
- During the team meeting at Stand-up - the QA Team Lead or/or the responsible person from the testers informs about the status of the tasks that have been transferred for testing - the Developer Team Lead or/or the responsible person from the developers.
- If there are corrections to the task from the testers, the developers take them to work. Distributes tasks among Fullstack participants - Team Lead developers.
6- After the changes are made, the developer moves the card to the “Ready for test” column and at the daily team meeting, the new task for testing is reported to the Team Lead to the testers and/or the responsible person from the testers.
7- See item No. 6.
8- 3-4 days before the presentation of the project - the Team Lead of Fullstack students transfers/sends the finished project for testing to the Team Lead of QA students.
9- 1-2 days before the presentation of the project, the team sends the finished project to the Frontend and Backend mentors for a general code review. IMPORTANTLY! Only if the project is checked by testers.
10- Frontend and Backend mentors provide feedback:
- If everything is OK, they allow the presentation of the project.
- If there are comments/corrections, students correct them and send them to mentors for rechecking.
11- The team (FS+QA students) is preparing for a joint presentation of the project:
- The Scrum Master prepares slides for the presentation (see Preparation materials for the project; be sure to present the entire team (students and FS and QA) in the "project participants" slide);
- the team rehearses the presentation;

Recommendations: the introductory part of the presentation is told by the Scrum Master of the team, then the project (live page) is presented by the Team Lead from FS, after which the floor is handed over to the QA Team Lead - the Team Lead tells about their part of the work on the project.

12- On the day of project protection, the team presents its project.

Kapusta

Work plan on a team project

1- Students are assigned to teams by the client-manager.
2- Each team gets a separate channel in Slack.
3- The team needs to complete the task:
- choose the name of the team;
- select a Team Lead;
- choose a Scrum Master;
- view project materials.
4- Attend the introductory webinar on "Project start. Planning with a mentor."
5- The Scrum Master needs to populate the Trello board and invite all team members to it.
6- The team needs to distribute tasks among all project participants.
7- Everyone works on their task and timely updates information about the task during its execution.
8- The team addresses the Team Lead with technical questions.
9- The Team Lead communicates with the mentor if there are issues that could not be resolved as a team.
10- The team conducts a daily Stand Up lasting 15-20 minutes.
11- Each tester on Stand Up answers three questions: What did he do yesterday (today)? What will be done today (tomorrow)? What do you need help with?

!!!Importantly!!! at Stand Up, we don't start analyzing the moments where help is needed, we talk about them so that the whole team understands how the process of completing the task goes. After the Stand Up, a tester who needs help can stay and discuss their questions with a team member who knows the answer or with the Team Lead.
